Welcome, Explorer!

This guide will walk you through setting up your Mini Satellite Ground Station. Your station will join a global network called TinyGS, allowing you to receive data from LoRa-enabled satellites orbiting Earth. The board provided is pre-flashed with the necessary firmware, but you can follow the steps below if you need to re-install it.

Your station operates in the 430-436 MHz frequency range.

1

Pre-Setup: TinyGS Telegram Bot

First, you need a Telegram account to communicate with your ground station via the TinyGS bot. This is how you'll get essential credentials.

  1. If you don't have it, install the Telegram app and create an account.
  2. Scan the QR code to open a chat with the "TinyGS Personal Bot".
  3. In the chat, type /start and send it.
  4. Next, type /mqtt and send it. The bot will reply with your unique MQTT credentials.
  5. Important: Keep these MQTT credentials safe and ready. You will need them in a later step.
2

Hardware Setup: Location

Find the Perfect Spot

The satellites you'll be tracking are in polar orbit. For the best signal, place your antenna in a location with an unobstructed view of the sky, especially towards the North and South horizons. You'll also need access to a power outlet and a stable Wi-Fi signal.

3

Firmware Installation (If Needed)

If you ever need to re-install the firmware, follow these simple steps.

  1. Press and hold the "BOOT" button on the receiver board.
  2. While still holding the BOOT button, connect the board to your computer with the USB cable. You can release the button after it's connected.
  3. Open a web browser (like Chrome or Edge) and go to: installer.tinygs.com
  4. Select the correct firmware for your board (e.g., "TTGO LoRa32 V1 433 Mhz").
  5. Click the "Install" button and follow the on-screen prompts to select your device's COM port.
  6. Wait for the installation process to complete. You'll see a success message when it's done.
4

Software Configuration

Now, let's configure your station to connect to the internet and the TinyGS network.
Crucially, the antenna must be connected BEFORE you power on the board.

  1. Power on the receiver board using the provided USB cable and a standard USB wall adapter. Slide the power switch to ON.
  2. On your phone or laptop, scan for Wi-Fi networks. Connect to the network named My TinyGS.
  3. Open a web browser and navigate to the address: http://192.168.4.1
  4. You'll see the TinyGS welcome page. Click on "Configure Parameters".
  5. Fill out the form exactly as described below. This is an interactive simulation of the actual page.

Configuration Portal (Simulation)

Board & Radio Settings (Do Not Change)

Ensure the following settings are selected. They are critical for correct operation.

  • Board Type: 433Mhz TTGO LoRa 32 v1
  • Enable TX: Unchecked
  • Automatic Tunning: Checked
  • Automatic Firmware Update: Checked
  • Board Template: Paste the exact string provided in the manual.

Configuration "saved"! Now, on the real device, go back to the home page and click Restart Station.

5

Go Live & Monitor

  1. After the station restarts, it will connect to your home Wi-Fi. You should get a notification from the Telegram bot saying your station is online!
  2. Connect your computer back to your home Wi-Fi. You can now access your station's dashboard by finding its IP address (the manual suggests trying 192.168.1.129, but this may vary).
  3. On the dashboard, verify the status. It should look like this:

MQTT Server CONNECTED

  1. If all statuses are green, you're good to go! Power down the station.
  2. Extend each telescopic antenna to exactly 33.8 cm. This is crucial for tuning to the correct frequency.
  3. Mount your station in its final, high-and-clear location and power it on.
  4. In your Telegram bot, type /weblogin to get a link to the main TinyGS website where you can monitor your station and see the packets you receive from space!

Important Tips & Cautions

  • ⚠️ Never transmit without a valid amateur radio license. Transmission without authorization is illegal in many jurisdictions and can interfere with critical communication systems.
  • Ensure that the antenna is always connected before powering on the ground station. Operating the radio without an antenna can damage the RF circuitry.
  • Avoid placing the antenna near metal objects such as grills, window bars, or metal poles. These can interfere with reception and detune the antenna.
  • Install the ground station in a location with maximum sky visibility, away from buildings, walls, or trees that can obstruct the satellite signal path.
  • Keep the unit protected from direct rain or excessive humidity. While outdoor use is encouraged, weatherproofing the installation is recommended.
  • Do not power the board from unstable power sources, such as USB ports on old laptops or power banks with poor regulation. Use a stable 5V USB adapter.
  • Always check the antenna connections for tightness and integrity. Loose U.FL or SMA connectors can cause signal loss.
  • Avoid handling the receiver board while powered, especially near the antenna connection, to reduce risk of electrostatic damage or short circuits.
  • If making custom firmware changes, ensure you back up current configurations and test carefully before uploading.
  • Regularly inspect the antenna for bends or corrosion that may impact performance.
